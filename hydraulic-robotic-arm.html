Step-by-Step Build

  1. Sketch degrees of freedom: Base (rotate or just lift), elbow, gripper. Keep it simple for the first build: base lift, elbow lift, and a simple pinch gripper.
  2. Cut parts:
    • Base: wide U-frame to support the shoulder pivot.
    • Upper arm ~15 cm; forearm ~15 cm; gripper 2 opposing jaws.
    • Pivots: Use skewers/bolts through reinforced holes (double-layer cardboard at pivots). Ensure free motion without wobble.
  3. Mount syringes: Pair each actuator: one control syringe (in your hand) connected by tube to one arm syringe. Place arm syringes so they push/pull at sensible lever arms (not too close to the joint).
  4. Tubing & fill: Fill lines completely (no bubbles) with colored water; secure to syringe nipples. Tape tubing along structure to avoid kinks.
  5. Gripper return: Add a small rubber band to reopen the gripper when fluid retracts.
  6. Test one axis at a time: Move the control syringe; look for leaks, flex, or binding. Reinforce as needed.

Tuning

  • Not enough lift? Increase lever arm or use a larger area slave syringe (more force).
  • Too little travel? Use a smaller slave or move the push-point farther from the joint.
  • Jittery motion? Purge tiny bubbles; lubricate pivots; stiffen cardboard near joints.
