Engineering Prep ‚Äî 3 Levers in Play

  • Mousetrap lever: longer boom = more string pulled (distance) with less torque.
  • Axle radius: smaller axle = more wraps (distance) with less torque at the wheel.
  • Wheel radius: larger wheel = more distance per axle turn but needs more torque.

Build Steps

  1. Base: Cut a straight base ~30√ó12 cm. Mark true centerlines for axles (accuracy prevents scrub).
  2. Axle mounts: Glue/tape two straws at front and rear, square to the centerline.
  3. Wheels: Fit wheels to axles; ensure minimal wobble; add rubber bands or balloons around rims for traction.
  4. Mount the mousetrap: Center it with the snap bar pointing backward. Secure firmly (screws/zip ties if possible).
  5. Attach the boom: Lash/glue a dowel to the trap‚Äôs snap bar, pointing rearward (length 30‚Äì50 cm to start).
  6. Drive string: Tie one end to the boom tip; route the string forward over a small guide and back to the rear axle; tie a slip loop that slides off when fully unwound (so the car can coast).
  7. Wind & launch: Rotate rear wheels to wind the string onto the axle while cocking the trap (careful!). Set down and release the trap to pull the string.

  • Doesn‚Äôt start / slips? Improve tire grip; clean the floor; reduce axle radius to up torque.
  • Launches fast then stops: Increase boom length or reduce axle radius for more total string length (distance).
  • Veers: Check axle parallelism; equalize wheel diameters; true any wobbly hubs.
  • Trap slams before unwinding fully: Use a multi-wrap path or add a small pulley at the boom tip to change the take-up rate.
