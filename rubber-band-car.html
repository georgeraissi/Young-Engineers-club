  5. Wind & release: Hold the rear wheels and rotate them backward to wind the band around the axle. Set down and let go.

Test & Troubleshoot (systematic)

  • Doesn‚Äôt move? Ensure the rubber band is truly attached to the axle (it must turn the axle, not slip). Tighten with a knot or a dab of glue.
  • Wheels spin in place? Add traction: stretch a thin rubber band around the CD rim or run a hot-glue bead around the edge. Try a rougher surface floor.
  • Veers left/right? Align straws; ensure wheel discs are parallel; equalize wheel diameters.
  • Short range? Use a longer/thicker band (more energy), larger rear wheels (more rollout per rev), and reduce axle friction.

Measure & Iterate (simple experiments)

  • Distance vs number of winds: Mark winds and plot distance.
  • Wheel size sweep: Try bottle caps vs CDs; compare rollout and traction.
  • Band types: Long thin vs short thick; note how stiffness k affects acceleration and range.
