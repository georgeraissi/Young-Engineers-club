2. Tools & Workspace Preparation

  • Soldering kit (soldering iron, fine tip, leaded solder, flux): To connect wires to your flight controller (FC) and electronic speed controller (ESC).
  • Wire cutters & strippers: To cut motor wires and prepare ends for soldering.
  • Precision screwdrivers (hex drivers 1.5 mm, 2 mm, Phillips): For assembling the frame and mounting components.
  • Tweezers or needle-nose pliers: Useful for holding tiny parts while soldering.
  • Heat-shrink tubing & electrical tape: For insulating wires and preventing shorts.
  • Smoke-stopper: A safety device to prevent frying your electronics on the first power-up.
  • Threadlocker (purple/blue): Prevents screws from loosening from vibration.
  • Digital scale: To ensure your build stays under 250g.

Tip: Set up a clean, well-lit desk with a silicone mat to protect your surface from solder burns.

  • Mount motors temporarily to confirm wire reach.

  • Secure with long M2 screws.
  • Plug in or solder the FPV system, ensuring no sharp bends in the camera cable.

Tip: Use threadlocker sparingly on screws to prevent vibration loosening.

8. Weight Management

  • Trim wires to the shortest safe length.
  • Use a lightweight buzzer or skip it if your receiver has a beeper mode.
  • Choose a 650 mAh battery if you‚Äôre close to the limit.
  • Consider switching to an AIO board if using separate FC/ESC adds too much weight.

9. First Power-Up (Smoke Stopper Test)

  • Connect the drone to a smoke stopper before plugging into a LiPo.
  • If the light stays green (or bulb doesn‚Äôt glow), your wiring is safe.
  • If it trips, disconnect immediately and re-inspect soldering.
  • Once it passes, connect the drone to your computer via USB.

10. Firmware Setup (Betaflight)

  • Install Betaflight Configurator (free software).
  • Connect drone via USB.
  • Flash latest stable firmware for your FC.
  • Calibrate accelerometer.
  • Set receiver protocol (e.g., ELRS).
  • Configure video system (digital/analog).
  • Test motor directions (use props OFF). Reverse any incorrect ones in Betaflight.
  • Set arming switch, beeper switch, and flight modes on your transmitter.

11. Propellers, Battery & Final Assembly

  • Install propellers (check correct rotation direction).
  • Strap battery securely with a rubberized strap + pad.
  • Re-check screws, cables, and antenna positions.
  • Weigh the finished build.

12. First Flight Test

  • Go to an open field.
  • Arm motors without props to ensure everything responds.
  • With props on, throttle up gently and hover at low altitude.
  • Check stick response and FPV feed.
  • If it drifts heavily, re-calibrate or check for mechanical issues.

13. Tuning & Improvements

  • Adjust rates (stick sensitivity) for smoother flying.
  • Experiment with different propellers (pitch and blade count) for flight style.
  • Record DVR footage and review flight characteristics.

14. Maintenance & Safety

  • Always discharge and store LiPos safely in fireproof bags.
  • Inspect screws and props before flights.
  • Keep antennas clear of props.
  • Clean frame and electronics if exposed to dirt or moisture.
